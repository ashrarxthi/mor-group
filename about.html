The people behind Mor

Two dancers,
one language

Aishwarya and Shrinali bring two of India's great classical forms — Kathak and Bharatanatyam — into one studio in the heart of New York City.

Aishwarya R. · Kathak
I
Aishwarya R.
Trained Kathak Dancer · Co-founder

Aishwarya has trained in Kathak since childhood, studying under senior gurus in the Lucknow gharana tradition. For her, Kathak is a language — one built on precision, rhythm, and the quiet power of a perfectly placed pause.

She has performed across India and the United States, and brings the same rigour she brings to the stage into every class. Her sessions emphasise the fundamentals: taal, footwork, spins, and the geometry of the body in space.

Aishwarya believes anyone can dance Kathak. The form rewards patience, and patience is something she has in abundance.

Kathak Lucknow Gharana Semi-Classical Taal & Footwork All Levels
Shrinali P. · Bharatanatyam
II
Shrinali P.
Trained Bharatanatyam Dancer · Co-founder

Shrinali's foundation is in Bharatanatyam — a form built on geometry, devotion, and the expressive power of abhinaya. She trained formally from a young age and went on to perform and teach across studios in India before moving to New York.

In her classes, she focuses on the connection between movement and meaning: every hand gesture, every gaze, every shift in weight is intentional. She brings that consciousness into sessions for all levels.

Shrinali also leads the semi-classical sessions, where classical vocabulary meets contemporary feeling — always rooted, always purposeful.

What we believe
The way we teach
One
Rooted in tradition

Kathak and Bharatanatyam are two of India's eight classical dance forms, with centuries of history. We teach their vocabulary with care and deep respect.

Two
Open to everyone

You don't need a background in dance to begin. Our beginner sessions welcome total newcomers — curiosity and an open mind are the only requirements.

Three
Community first

Dance is a shared language. We build a space where students connect, grow together, and celebrate the joy of movement — individually and as one.
